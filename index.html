Cost Analysis

Top 3 Most Expensive Majors in Australia

This visualization reveals the highest-cost programs for international students studying in Australia. The average total estimated costs include tuition fees, living expenses, and other associated costs measured in thousands of AUD.

Programs such as Biotechnology, Climate Science, and Computer Engineering consistently rank as the most expensive due to extended study durations, specialized facilities, and practical training requirements. Understanding these costs is crucial for prospective students planning their education investment.

Employment

States with Highest Employment Rates

This chart highlights the top 3 Australian states with the highest employment-to-population ratios, indicating where international students are most likely to find work opportunities.

States like Australian Capital Territory, Northern Territory, and Western Australia often lead due to strong economies, labor shortages in key industries, and favorable employment conditions for graduates.

Regional Distribution

Cost Comparison Across States

International student costs vary significantly across different Australian states. This visualization compares the average total costs including tuition and living expenses.

Cities like Sydney and Melbourne typically have higher costs and low employability due to accommodation prices and urban living expenses, while regional areas may offer more affordable options with quality education and higher employment opportunity.

Trend Analysis

Cost Trends by City

This time-series visualization tracks how education costs have evolved across different program categories over recent years. The trend lines reveal patterns in tuition inflation and cost growth rates.
